Risk Signals & Actionable Insights
Priority Issues
🔴
Sales events creating misaligned clients at scale
5 clients cancelled citing they didn't understand what Lucro was at signup — primarily from webinar/BPA events. One didn't know Lucro takes over bookkeeping; another didn't know QuickBooks was required. These are entirely preventable exits at the top of the funnel, representing the largest single preventable cohort.
🔴
CPA takeovers are the #1 churn driver — no counter-strategy exists
37% of all cancellations (10 of 27) cite a new CPA or bookkeeper taking over. This is a structural competitive threat. There is currently no apparent retention protocol for CPA-introduction scenarios, and no data suggesting Lucro is positioning itself as complementary (rather than competitive) to CPAs.
🟠
Onboarding churn is accelerating — 9 of 16 January exits were in-onboarding
9 of 16 January exits (56%) occurred during onboarding — clients who never fully activated. Multiple records show "No Recent Interactions" even during onboarding, pointing to a systemic engagement gap where clients drop out before Lucro ever has a chance to deliver value.
🟠
Value proposition is not landing — "insights not relevant" is a recurring complaint
Several clients (Druckman, Kreutz, Cornish, Annis) explicitly said they already knew what Lucro was showing them, or that the dashboard didn't meet expectations. This points to a gap between what sales promises and what delivery provides, especially for more financially literate clients.
🟡
Internal bookkeeper transitions are causing preventable churn
At least one client (Collaborative Medical) cancelled specifically because an internal Lucro bookkeeper transition was handled poorly. Smooth internal handoffs should be a retention-critical process — this is entirely within Lucro's control.

S1 Add an explicit product explainer to all mass sales events (webinars, BPA). Clearly communicate that Lucro takes over bookkeeping and that a QuickBooks account is required before signup. Sales Misalignment High Low

Onboarding
#ActionAddressesImpactEffort
O1 90-day email connection strategy is in place (monthly touchpoints for 3 months post-onboarding). Audit whether touchpoints are driving replies, not just sends — flag any account that misses a check-in for immediate outreach. Onboarding Churn High In Progress
O2 Set a mandatory onboarding activation milestone — no client should go more than 14 days without a logged interaction. Escalate unresponsive accounts to the Onboarding Manager before day 21. Onboarding Churn High Low
O3 Improve the onboarding value demonstration — ensure clients see a meaningful insight or financial win within the first 30 days to reduce "lack of value" early exits. Lack of Value Medium Medium
Account Management
#ActionAddressesImpactEffort
A1 Introduce a CPA co-introduction call for any client who mentions a new CPA or bookkeeper. Position Lucro's reporting and insights as additive to what the CPA does, not duplicative. CPA Takeover High Low
A2 Develop a retention save script specifically for financial burden cases — offer a pause, discount, or reduced-scope plan before a cancellation is confirmed. Financial Burden Medium Low
A3 Schedule quarterly business reviews for all long-term clients to proactively surface value before dissatisfaction builds — particularly for financially literate practices. Lack of Value Medium Medium
Operations
#ActionAddressesImpactEffort
P1 Create a formal internal bookkeeper transition protocol — include a client-facing communication template, a handoff checklist, and a post-transition satisfaction check within 7 days. Internal Handoffs Medium Low
P2 An exit survey is already in place for cancelling clients. Focus on improving completion rates — currently ~4% of exits have unclear reasoning, suggesting low survey uptake. Consider adding a personal follow-up prompt to encourage responses without making it compulsory. Data Gaps Medium Low
Leadership & Strategy
#ActionAddressesImpactEffort
