Risk Signals & Actionable Insights
Priority Issues
🔴
Sales events creating misaligned clients at scale
5 clients cancelled citing they didn't understand what Lucro was at signup — primarily from webinar/BPA events. One didn't know Lucro takes over bookkeeping; another didn't know QuickBooks was required. These are entirely preventable exits at the top of the funnel, representing the largest single preventable cohort.
🔴
CPA takeovers are the #1 churn driver — no counter-strategy exists
37% of all cancellations (10 of 27) cite a new CPA or bookkeeper taking over. This is a structural competitive threat. There is currently no apparent retention protocol for CPA-introduction scenarios, and no data suggesting Lucro is positioning itself as complementary (rather than competitive) to CPAs.
🟠
Onboarding churn is accelerating — 9 of 16 January exits were in-onboarding
9 of 16 January exits (56%) occurred during onboarding — clients who never fully activated. Multiple records show "No Recent Interactions" even during onboarding, pointing to a systemic engagement gap where clients drop out before Lucro ever has a chance to deliver value.
🟠
Value proposition is not landing — "insights not relevant" is a recurring complaint
Several clients (Druckman, Kreutz, Cornish, Annis) explicitly said they already knew what Lucro was showing them, or that the dashboard didn't meet expectations. This points to a gap between what sales promises and what delivery provides, especially for more financially literate clients.
🟡
Internal bookkeeper transitions are causing preventable churn
At least one client (Collaborative Medical) cancelled specifically because an internal Lucro bookkeeper transition was handled poorly. Smooth internal handoffs should be a retention-critical process — this is entirely within Lucro's control.
